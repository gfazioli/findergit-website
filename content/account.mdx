# Account

The **Account** view turns FinderGit into a window onto your GitHub profile — avatar, bio, the numbers that matter, and a full year of contributions — without leaving the app or opening a browser.

![The Account dashboard — profile header, stat tiles, and the contribution graph](/screenshot-hero-account.png)

## Your profile at a glance

The header pulls your GitHub identity: avatar, name, `@login`, bio, company, location, website, and the year you joined — with a button straight to your profile page.

## The numbers

A grid of stat tiles covering both your social proof and your activity:

- **Followers / Following**
- **Stars** — summed across your repositories
- **Public Repos / Private Repos** — your own non-fork repositories
- **Forks** — how many of your repos are forks
- **Pull Requests** (with how many merged) and **Issues** opened
- **Commits** in the last year, and the number of repositories you **contributed** to
- **Organizations** you belong to

<Callout type="info" emoji="🔒">
Because FinderGit runs entirely on your Mac with your own GitHub sign-in, it can show your **private** numbers too — private repo counts and the like — that a public profile page hides.
</Callout>

## Contributions, two ways

A full 52-week contribution calendar, with month and weekday labels just like GitHub. Use the toggle in the top-right to switch between the classic **heatmap grid** and a **line graph** of daily contributions over the year:

![The contribution panel switched to the line-graph view](/screenshot-account-graph.png)

## Top repositories, languages & organizations

Below the graph: your most-starred repositories, a breakdown of the languages you work in, and the organizations you're part of.

## New-star notifications

FinderGit can keep an eye on your repositories while it's running and let you know the moment one earns a star — naming the repository, not just bumping a number.

- A pulsing **(+) badge** appears on the **Stars** tile whenever a repo gains a star; click it for a popover listing exactly which repositories were starred, and click through to open any of them.
- Optionally, enable **desktop notifications** in **Settings → Git → Star Notifications** (off by default) and pick how often to check (every 10 / 30 / 60 minutes). The in-app badge works either way — turning notifications off doesn't hide your new stars.

<Callout type="info" emoji="💡">
The badge and notifications share one baseline, so you're never told about the same star twice — and a relaunch won't re-announce stars earned while the app was closed.
</Callout>
